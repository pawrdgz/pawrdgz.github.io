Space Travel

    2020

  • motive

    Project for my MFA in Digital Experience Design

  • tags

    UI/UX Design, Website Design

Space Travel was conceived as a project for the Master in Digital Design Experiences (BAU). It is a landing page website for both mobile phone and desktop. Texts were given to us and we were asked to do all the visual process, excluding the previous data recollection and UX study that would recquire a full project of its own. Space Travel is supposed to work as a landing page for a fictional travel agency that specializes in luxury trips to Mars.

Since we were given the content, the first step was to locate and structure the landing page into wireframes.

I normally made them by hand and then project that into Miro, as it can be easily shared with others.

Next, I started to search for inspiration that could help me set the mood. Since this was space-related, I searched for cyberpunk films, dystopian scenarios, neo noir aesthetics and art from Moebius. Although the final product didn't quite reach this futuristic aesthetic, it held resemblance with some films that I researched (mostly Blade Runner and Ad Astra).

Here is a photo of a small part of my moodboard.

Once I knew what I wanted, I started to play with the colors and fonts to set the typography, typescale and pallette. Here are some tests that I did before deciding which one I was going to use.

Since I am a very visual person, I always need to check possibilities side by side.

The ones with the star are the ones picked.

As shown, I had different options for the color pallette, but the one that convinced me the most was the one closest to our imaginery of Mars, and since it was a landing page, I wanted it to be easily distinguishable.

The ones with the star are once again the final choice.
